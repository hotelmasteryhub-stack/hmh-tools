Room Cost Calculator
What does it cost to run this hotel per room sold? Enter all costs — fixed and variable — and know your required room rate.
WHAT THIS TOOL DOES

This tool calculates the full cost of running your hotel per room sold — not just housekeeping, but every cost category: rent, payroll, utilities, laundry, maintenance, and any other fixed or variable cost. It tells you what each room night sold actually costs the business, and what rate you need to charge to cover those costs at your target margin.

The occupancy sensitivity table shows how your required room rate changes at different occupancy levels — so you can see what happens to your pricing if a slow month brings you to 40% occupancy instead of 70%.

The cost per room figure from this tool feeds the Stay Cost Calculator — which uses it as the base cost for custom stay pricing per room type.

HOW TO USE — THREE STEPS
1
Setup — enter hotel details and all costs
Enter total rooms, occupancy %, days in month, and target margin. Then add every monthly cost — each as a separate line. Mark each as Fixed (same every month) or Variable (changes with occupancy). Add and remove lines freely.
2
Cost Per Room — see your required rate
Full breakdown of every cost line as monthly total, per day, per sold room, and % of total. Fixed vs variable split. Required room price = cost per room ÷ (1 − margin %).
3
Sensitivity — see what happens at different occupancy
The sensitivity table shows required room rate at 30% to 100% occupancy. Fixed costs spread over fewer rooms at low occupancy — your required rate rises. Know your floor before setting seasonal pricing.
4
History — track this month to month
If you run this monthly, save each month's Setup to History before making changes for the next one — it never touches or clears what's currently on screen. Compare Cost Per Room and Required Price across months at a glance, or load an old month back in.

ONE RULE

If you do not know what each sold room costs to run, you cannot set a room rate with confidence. Know your number. Know it by occupancy level. Then price from knowledge — not instinct.
