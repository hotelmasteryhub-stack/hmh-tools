WHAT THIS TOOL DOES

This tool gives you the full monthly F&B profit and loss. It answers the question the shift planner cannot: is the F&B operation actually profitable this month, after every cost?

Revenue lines, purchasing costs, staff roles, and overheads are all fully dynamic — add and remove lines to match exactly how your operation is structured. No forced categories.

It calculates the four KPIs that matter most: food cost %, beverage cost %, labour cost %, and net profit % — each benchmarked so you know if you are in range.

FOUR KPIs — WHAT TO WATCH
Food Cost % Benchmark: 28–35%
Total food purchasing ÷ total food revenue. Above 35% means pricing is too low, portions too large, or waste is high.
Beverage Cost % Benchmark: 18–25%
Total beverage purchasing ÷ total beverage revenue. Beverages carry higher margins than food — above 28% signals over-pouring or poor pricing.
Labour Cost % Benchmark: 30–35%
Total staff cost ÷ total revenue. Must include total employer cost — not just gross wages. Above 40% means overstaffed relative to revenue.
Net Profit % Benchmark: 8–15%
Net profit ÷ total revenue. After all costs. Below 5% is very thin — any slow month puts you in loss.
HOW TO USE

Setup — do once per month
Enter hotel name, month, year, currency, and toggle which services ran this month.
2
Revenue — add lines per service
Each active service has its own list of revenue lines. Add as many as you need — Buffet, À la carte, Room service, Wine, Cocktails. Enter the actual amount for each line. Covers field is per service.
3
Costs — add purchasing, staff roles, overheads
Three dynamic sections: purchasing costs (food and beverage by category), staff roles (each role as a separate line, total employer cost), and fixed overheads. Add and remove lines freely.
4
P&L Report — read and act
Full P&L with all KPIs and benchmarks. Export as PDF for monthly review or email to your accountant.

ONE RULE

A busy restaurant is not the same as a profitable restaurant. Know your food cost %, labour cost %, and net profit every month. These three numbers tell you the truth.
