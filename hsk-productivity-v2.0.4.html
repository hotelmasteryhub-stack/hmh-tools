HSK Productivity Tracker
Monthly housekeeping productivity report. Know if your team is performing — not just working.
WHAT THIS TOOL DOES

This tool tells you whether your housekeeping team is working efficiently — not just how many hours they worked, but whether those hours match what the rooms and tasks they actually did should have taken.

The primary KPI is Team Overhour % — the difference between expected hours (built automatically from what was tracked each day) and actual hours worked. If your team took 10% more hours than expected, that is your overhour %. Below 5% is good. Above 10% needs review.

Productivity is a daily measure, not a month-end guess. This tool is built around tracking it every day, so the month's numbers are real — not whatever anyone can remember weeks later.

FOUR STEPS
1
Setup tab — enter rooms, cleaning types, and staff (once)
List every room and cleaning space, every cleaning type with its target minutes, and your staff roster. This is the one-time setup everything else is built from.
2
Today tab — track the day, every day
For each employee: add every room/space they cleaned with its cleaning type (target minutes fill in automatically), any extra ad-hoc tasks, and their actual hours worked today from your timesheet. Click SAVE TODAY TO LOG.
3
Staff Data & KPI Report — build themselves
Nothing to type here. Expected and Actual Hrs are computed automatically from every day logged so far this month. Use the global Reporting Period selector above the workspace to review any month without changing Setup.
4
KPI Report tab — read and share
Team overhour %, adjusted rate (core team only), top performer, and most overhours — all live. Click the small ? next to any KPI for a plain-English explanation. Export as PDF or email the headline numbers to a GM. Click ★ SAVE TO HISTORY once a month is final — it archives a permanent snapshot browsable from the History tab.
KEY CONCEPTS
Expected hours
The standard target time for everything an employee actually cleaned this month, summed automatically from every day logged on the Today tab — never typed in.
Actual hours
The real hours worked, entered daily on the Today tab from your timesheet or payroll, and summed automatically across the month.
Diff % per person
(Actual − Expected) ÷ Expected. Negative = faster than target (green). Positive = slower than target (red).
Team Overhour % ★ Primary KPI
Total actual ÷ total expected − 1. Below 5%: good. 5–10%: monitor. Above 10%: review with the team.
Adjusted Overhour % (Core Team)
Same calculation but excluding staff flagged N (new) or V (volunteer). Gives a cleaner view of your experienced team's performance.
New / Volunteer flags
N = new staff in training. V = volunteer or non-payroll worker. Their hours are tracked but excluded from the adjusted KPI so training drag does not hide your core team's performance.
HISTORY ARCHIVE

There are two different histories here, on purpose. The daily log (built from the Today tab) is the real day-by-day record everything is computed from. ★ SAVE TO HISTORY on the KPI Report tab is a separate, occasional action — it archives a permanent, final snapshot of a whole month once you're confident it's done, for year-over-year comparison. The History tab shows every month archived this way, a Team Overhour % trend chart, and your best and worst months.

ONE RULE

A housekeeper can be busy all day and still be underperforming. Hours alone do not tell you this. Overhour % does. Track it every month.
