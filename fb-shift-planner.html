WHAT THIS TOOL DOES — AND WHAT IT DOES NOT

This tool answers one question: for the staff I am putting on this shift and the covers I expect to sell, does this service break even on its daily fixed cost share and labour cost?

It is a shift planning and break-even tool — not a full F&B profit and loss statement. It does not track monthly food purchasing costs, beverage stock, or full salary overheads. For the complete monthly F&B profitability picture, use the F&B Monthly P&L tool.

Use this tool before each service to decide: how many staff do I need? Is it worth running the full service today? What is the minimum covers to cover costs?

KEY CONCEPTS
Real labour cost per hour
What the employee earns is not what the business pays. Add employer social contributions, holiday pay accrual, pension, and insurance — typically 20–35% on top of gross pay. Enter the gross hourly rate and the employer cost multiplier in Setup. The tool uses the real cost in all calculations.
Contribution per labour hour ★ Primary KPI
Total contribution (revenue minus food cost) divided by total labour hours. Must exceed your real cost per hour for the shift to pay for its labour. Industry benchmark: 2–3× real hourly rate for a healthy shift.
Fixed cost share per service
Your monthly fixed costs (manager salary, other salaried staff) divided by 30 days and 4 services. This is what each service must contribute before it is truly breaking even.
Break-even covers
The minimum covers you need to sell in this service, with this staffing, to cover all costs. Share this with your team before the service starts. It converts the financial target into a simple operational goal.
Replacement ratio
If a guest chooses the cheaper product instead of the premium one, how many extra units do you need to sell to break even? Above 3× is a risk signal — your product mix matters.
HOW TO USE — DAILY WORKFLOW
1
Setup — do this once, update when prices change
Enter fixed monthly costs, gross hourly rate and employer cost multiplier per service, and your products with selling price and food cost. The real cost per hour calculates automatically.
2
Before each shift — enter staff and expected covers
Enter kitchen and floor staff count and shift hours. Enter how many covers you expect. See your break-even, shift result, and contribution per labour hour before the service starts.
3
After each shift — update actual covers sold
Replace expected with actual covers sold per product. All KPIs recalculate instantly — compare planned vs actual.
4
End of day — check the Daily Summary
All 4 services side by side. Net daily result, total covers, avg contribution per cover. Copy the net result into the Weekly Tracker.
5
Weekly Tracker — enter daily totals
Enter each day's net result, covers, and labour hours. Best and worst day, days profitable, and weekly averages calculate automatically.
EXPORT OPTIONS
CSVExport all shift data

Downloads setup, all four service inputs and calculated KPIs, and weekly tracker as a .csv file. Good for monthly archives or sharing with an accountant.

✉Email a daily summary

Sends a plain text daily summary — net result per service, total covers, contribution per labour hour. Good for updating a GM or owner at end of day.

PDFPrint the full report

Prints all tabs — setup, all service shifts, daily summary, and weekly tracker. Good for a weekly review or printed monthly record.

BACKUP ↓Save everything as a JSON file

Downloads a complete backup of all setup, service inputs, and weekly tracker. Store it somewhere safe. Restore from this file if your browser data is cleared or you switch devices.

RESTORE ↑Load from a backup file

Select a .json backup to restore all data. You will be asked to confirm before anything is replaced.

ONE RULE

Know your break-even before the service starts. A full dining room means nothing if the shift is losing money. Share the cover target with your team every day.
