WHAT THIS TOOL DOES

This tool helps you build a structured onboarding programme for every new receptionist. It covers the systems they need access to, what they need to learn at the front desk, and tracks their progress from observation to working independently.

Customise it once for your hotel in Edit mode. Then use it for every new starter.

TWO MODES
EDIT mode
Customise everything for your hotel. Edit task descriptions, add your own tasks, delete ones that don't apply, rename categories, and update the access checklist to reflect your systems and processes.
USE mode
Track the trainee's progress. Set the stage per task — Observation, Guided, Independent, or Signed off. Add buddy sign-off and date. Progress bar updates automatically.
HOW TO GET STARTED
1
Go to EDIT mode
Work through each section. Edit task descriptions to match your hotel's systems. The receptionist role is the most complex — make sure the tasks reflect your actual PMS, payment system, and OTA setup.
2
Complete the Access Checklist first
A receptionist cannot do their job without system access. Submit every request on Day 1 — PMS login, card terminal PIN, OTA extranet access, hotel email. These take time to set up.
3
Assign buddies per shift
Reception covers multiple shifts. The morning buddy and evening buddy may be different people. Enter both in the trainee profile. Brief them on the tool before the new starter arrives.
4
Switch to USE mode on Day 1
Work through tasks together. Set the stage for each one as training progresses. A task only reaches Signed Off when the trainee can do it independently.
5
Complete final sign-off and save as PDF
When all tasks are signed off, complete Section D and save as PDF. Keep it as a training record for this staff member.
EXPORT OPTIONS
PDF
Prints the full onboarding document — trainee profile, access checklist, all tasks with progress stages, and the final sign-off section.
CSV
Downloads all tasks with their current stage as a spreadsheet. Good for keeping a record of completed onboardings per staff member.
✉ EMAIL
Sends a progress summary — how many tasks signed off and which ones are still pending. Good for updating a manager who is off site.
ONE RULE

A task is only signed off when the trainee can do it independently — not just when they have been shown it. The reception role carries real responsibility from day one. Don't rush the sign-off.
