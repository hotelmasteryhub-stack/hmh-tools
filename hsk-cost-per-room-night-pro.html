WHAT THIS TOOL DOES

This tool gives you the full monthly HSK cost picture — not just one number, but a complete breakdown. It calculates the two industry-standard metrics: CPOR (Cost Per Occupied Room — what each sold room costs in housekeeping) and CPAR (Cost Per Available Room — your fixed HSK burden regardless of occupancy).

It separates your fixed wages (manager, permanent staff — paid regardless of occupancy) from variable wages (casual, agency — scale with occupancy). This distinction matters because at low occupancy, fixed costs drive your CPOR up — not inefficiency.

The room mix setup pulls your design cost per room type from the HSK Room Cost Calculator and auto-calculates a weighted average design cost. Every month you see the gap between what HSK should cost by design and what it actually cost.

KEY METRICS EXPLAINED
CPOR — Cost Per Occupied Room ★ Industry standard
Total HSK cost ÷ rooms occupied. The primary benchmark. Used by hotel chains worldwide to compare HSK efficiency. Benchmark: €8–€20 depending on room type mix and service level.
CPAR — Cost Per Available Room
Total HSK cost ÷ total rooms available (all rooms × all days). Shows your HSK cost burden regardless of occupancy. Useful for understanding fixed cost exposure at different occupancy levels.
Fixed vs Variable cost split
Fixed wages (manager, permanent staff) do not change with occupancy. Variable wages (casual, agency) do. At 40% occupancy your CPOR spikes — fixed wages spread over fewer rooms. This is why the split matters.
Weighted design cost
A weighted average of your design cost per room type based on your room mix. Comes from the HSK Room Cost Calculator. Update it when cleaning targets, linen policy, or staff rates change.
HSK % of ADR
CPOR ÷ ADR × 100. How much of every euro of room revenue goes to housekeeping. Benchmark: 15–25% for most independent hotels.
HOW TO USE — WORKFLOW
1
Setup — do once, update when structure changes
Enter hotel name, manager, total rooms. Build your room mix table — add each room type, enter units and design cost per night from the HSK Room Cost Calculator. Weighted average calculates automatically. Add wage roles — mark each as Fixed or Variable.
2
This Month — enter actual costs each month
Select month and year. Enter room nights sold — occupancy % calculates automatically from your total rooms in Setup. Enter ADR. Enter actual wage cost per role, laundry invoice, and supplies. All KPIs calculate instantly.
3
Save to History
Click SAVE TO HISTORY at the end of each month. The 12-month history tab shows your trend, YTD totals, best and worst month, and trend direction.
4
Act on the gap
If CPOR is above your weighted design cost, investigate which component is driving it — is it wages, laundry, or supplies? Is occupancy low (fixed cost spread thin) or is actual usage above the design target?

ONE RULE

A rising CPOR in a high-occupancy month means costs are out of control. A rising CPOR in a low-occupancy month means fixed costs are spreading thin — that is a pricing problem, not an efficiency problem. Know which one it is before you act.
